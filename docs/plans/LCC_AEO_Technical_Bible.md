## SECTION 1: THE CORE GLOSSARY

_Standard SEO is about links. Modern AEO is about certainty._

### 01. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

**The Definition:** The practice of optimizing content so that "Answer Engines" (Siri, Alexa, Google Search Generative Experience) can serve a direct answer to a user query without them needing to click a link.
**Why it matters for Bars:** If a guest asks, "Does [Bar Name] have a pool table?" AEO ensures the answer is "Yes" on the screen, not "Click here to see our amenities."

### 02. AIO (AI Optimization)

**The Definition:** Preparing and structuring data specifically for Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini.
**Why it matters for Bars:** LLMs don't "browse" the web in real-time like humans; they rely on stored "knowledge." AIO ensures the underlying "facts" about your venue are so clear that the AI feels confident including you in its training weights and citations.

### 03. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

**The Definition:** A subset of optimization focused on improving visibility within AI-generated responses (like Perplexity or ChatGPT Search).
**Why it matters for Bars:** GEO focuses on the core "Trust Signals" that make an AI choose to cite _you_ as the #1 recommendation over the competitor three blocks away.

### 04. Entities vs. Keywords

**The Concept:** A "Keyword" is a string of text (e.g., "Martini"). An "Entity" is a unique, defined object with known attributes (e.g., "The Martini at The Varnish, which costs $16 and contains Plymouth Gin").
**The Shift:** We move bars from being "words on a page" to "Entities in the Global Knowledge Graph."

### 05. Structured Data & Schema.org

**The Concept:** A precise, code-based language (JSON-LD) that tells robots exactly what they are looking at.
**Hospitality Use-Case:** `Restaurant` schema allows you to define `servesCuisine`, `priceRange`, `menu`, and `acceptsReservations` in 100% machine-readable code.

## SECTION 2: THE "ZERO-CLICK" THREAT

_58%+ of mobile searches result in zero clicks to a website._

A "Zero-Click" search is a win for the user and a loss for a standard agency. If the guest finds your hours and menu on Google and then drives to your bar, you made money, but your website didn't get a "hit."
**Technical Strategy:** We embrace the Zero-Click by ensuring our **Entity Proximity** (Maps) and **Rich Snippets** (Menu data) are so dominant that the guest converts on the spot.

## SECTION 3: BEST PRACTICES FOR AI DOMINANCE

### 1. The "Standard Bearer" JSON-LD Implementation

Every hospitality site must have nested Schema. Do not just use a "General Business" tag.

- **Menu Schema:** Every dish must be its own `MenuItem` entity with price and ingredients.
- **LocalBusiness Schema:** Must include `geo` coordinates (Latitude/Longitude) to the 6th decimal point.
- **OpeningHoursSpecification:** Define special holiday hours and "Last Call" times explicitly.

### 2. Semantic Content Hierarchy

Stop writing "About Us" pages that read like novels. AI looks for headers.

- **Use H2s as Questions:** "What is the vibe at [Bar Name]?" -> Followed by a concise, factual 2-sentence answer.
- **The "Snippet" Paragraph:** The first 150 characters of any section should contain the core "fact" you want the AI to repeat.

### 3. Entity Cohesion (Citations)

AI builds a "Trust Score" by checking if your data matches across the web.

- **The NAP Standard:** Your **N**ame, **A**ddress, and **P**hone number must be identical on your website, Google Business, Apple Maps, Instagram, and even the "Contact" page of your host stand.
- **Social Signalling:** AI uses your Instagram and Yelp tags as "adjectives" for your entity. If your Instagram is all "dark cocktails" and your website says "family friendly," the AI gets confused and lowers your recommendation score.

### 4. Evidence of Life (The Review Loop)

Review sentiment is now used as a ranking factor for LLMs.

- **Attribute Mining:** AI reads reviews to find attributes you didn't list (e.g., "The bartenders are really fast").
- **Response Strategy:** Professional, keyword-rich responses to reviews (AEO-optimized) confirm to the AI that the entity is active and high-quality.

### 5. Mobile Speed as a Proxy for Quality

AI engines prioritize "Low Friction" answers. If your site takes 4 seconds to load a PDF menu, the AI considers you a "Bad Answer" and will hide you from the user.

- **The Fix:** HTML-first menus, zero autoplay video, and next-gen image formats (WebP/AVIF).
